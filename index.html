About Me

Hi! I'm David and I'm a final year undergraduate student at Texas A&M University at College Station. I am majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Game Design & Development and working as a student research assistant advised by Dr. Jong-in Lee as part of the SIID lab. I have an interest in fullstack development in software engineering as well as augmented/virtual reality development and computer graphics.

Research

Advised by Dr. Edgar Rojas-Muñoz, I contributed to the development of BurnCareXR, an immersive mixed reality system aimed at training medical personnel in burn patient care. My role involved implementing computer vision and augmented reality technologies to enable real-time tracking of a 3D hand model, ensuring seamless alignment with a real hand. This required proficiency in Unity and ManoMotion to develop an efficient and responsive system for accurate motion tracking.

Projects

Mess Waffles Point-of-Sale Website

Developed a web application for restaurant operations using Java, Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, and React. The platform supports role-based access, allowing customers to order, employees to manage orders, and managers to view reports and update the user base. Built a REST API for database interactions and implemented secure authentication with OAuth 2.0 and Google’s authorization server.

Pokémon AR

Pokémon AR is an augmented reality mystery game developed using Unity and Vuforia SDK for iOS and Android. Players solve puzzles and uncover clues in a Pokémon-themed universe, featuring interactive gameplay mechanics and event-based object activation programmed in C#. The game integrates 3D models, animations, and sound effects to create immersive AR environments that enhance player immersion.

Ray Tracer

Created a Ray Tracer built in C++ using OpenGL to simulate realistic lighting and visual effects. It features Blinn-Phong shading, multiple light sources, shadows, and reflections, enabling the creation of dynamic and visually rich scenes. Custom-designed scenes demonstrate the ray tracer's capabilities in rendering lifelike graphics and advanced lighting techniques.
